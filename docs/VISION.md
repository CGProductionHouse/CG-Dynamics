# CG Dynamics Product Vision

**Status:** Current product truth  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-20

## Product definition

CG Dynamics is the AI-assisted operating system for CG Production House. It should bring operations, client service, content production, marketing intelligence and specialist AI assistance into one coherent environment that is easier to use than the disconnected tools it replaces.

The system must reduce work rather than create more administration. Every interaction should leave the system smarter without forcing staff or clients to repeat information.

## Product outcome

CG Dynamics should eventually replace redundant daily systems where it can do so more clearly and pleasantly, especially fragmented task management, content planning, client approvals, reporting and operational handovers.

It is not merely a task manager, dashboard or generic chatbot. Its value comes from combining:

- Operational workflow.
- Client intelligence.
- Trusted marketing knowledge.
- Industry-specific specialist agents.
- Client-specific evolving memory.
- Content planning, approvals and production learning.
- Automated assistance that removes repetitive work.

## Primary product divisions

### Operations Hub

The internal execution environment for:

- Planner-style tasks and buckets.
- Client requests.
- Morning lists and daily work.
- Staff assignments.
- Operational calendars.
- Shoots, meetings, deadlines and content runs.
- Production workflows and handovers.

CG Dynamics should not replace Microsoft Teams by copying it poorly. It must become genuinely easier, clearer and more enjoyable to use.

### Client Intelligence

The premium client-facing environment for:

- Simple dashboards.
- Operational calendar visibility where appropriate.
- Client Schedule and scheduled content.
- Digital Content Guides.
- Approvals, comments and amendments.
- Reports and campaign performance.
- Recommendations and next actions.
- A dedicated client assistant backed by the correct industry and client knowledge.

The client portal should feel like the easiest business app the client has ever used.

### AI Workforce

A specialist knowledge and agent system built on trusted human sources and measured business data.

The intended hierarchy is:

1. Shared CG marketing and business knowledge.
2. Industry Brains.
3. Client Brains.
4. Campaign and Content Guide context.
5. Content Run outcomes.
6. Performance learning.

Industry Brains contain reusable, researched intelligence for sectors such as entertainment, hospitality, retail, architecture and other active client industries.

Each active client has a Client Brain that inherits relevant industry knowledge and adds:

- Brand identity.
- Products and services.
- Audience.
- Tone and voice.
- Approved and rejected wording.
- Content pillars.
- Staff and personalities.
- Previous campaigns.
- Competitors.
- Assets.
- Client preferences.
- Past approvals, amendments and disapprovals.
- Measured performance and operational learnings.

The AI Workforce must help produce human-sounding marketing. It should apply verified human knowledge, client context and real results instead of producing generic AI language.

## Digital Content Guides

PDF content guidelines must be replaced by first-class digital Content Guides inside CG Dynamics.

A Content Guide should be linked to:

- The client.
- The operational content run.
- The internal CG Calendar event.
- The client-visible operational calendar event where appropriate.
- Assigned staff.
- Approvals and comments.
- The relevant Industry Brain and Client Brain.

Each guide may contain structured ideas such as:

- Concept.
- Hook.
- Objective.
- Scene timeline.
- Dialogue.
- Voice-over.
- On-screen text.
- Shot requirements.
- Props.
- Locations.
- Talent.
- Call to action.
- Caption direction.
- Client comments and approval state.

The intended workflow is:

Idea capture -> AI draft -> Internal review -> Client review -> Approved -> Shoot -> Editing -> Client Schedule -> Performance learning.

AI suggestions must consider relevant researched opportunities, including seasonal events and public holidays, but only when appropriate to that client and industry.

## Content Run learning

After a content run, the assigned employee should use one simple voice note rather than completing a form.

The AI should interpret the voice note and automatically classify:

- Videos completed or shot.
- Videos amended on site.
- Videos rejected or no longer appropriate.
- Videos approved but not completed.
- Ideas to carry into the next content run.
- Time-sensitive ideas that must not be carried forward after their relevance expires.
- New client preferences and useful operational learnings.

The app should then show a short review summary for correction only if needed. The staff member should not be required to manually re-enter the information.

## Calendar and schedule boundaries

CG Calendar is for operational events, including:

- Meetings.
- Shoots.
- Content runs.
- Deadlines.
- Operational commitments.

Client Schedule owns:

- Scheduled posts.
- Monthly deliverables.
- Content publishing dates and times.

Client Schedule must default to a real calendar view where users can see days, dates and scheduled times. Board or bucket views may exist only as optional alternative views.

## AI philosophy

AI must retrieve, reason and apply expertise. It must not invent expertise.

Trusted knowledge should come from sources such as:

- Human experts.
- Classic advertising and copywriting sources.
- Behavioural psychology and consumer research.
- Peer-reviewed research.
- Official platform documentation.
- Reputable market data.
- South African market intelligence.
- Internal campaign performance.
- Approved client knowledge.
- Clearly labelled staff observations.

AI-generated text is not evidence and must never be treated as an authoritative source.

## Experience standard

The product should be:

- Mobile-friendly.
- Clear at first glance.
- Fast to operate.
- Simple for clients.
- Low-admin for staff.
- Premium and readable.
- Consistent across internal and client-facing areas.

The governing question for every feature is:

> Does this remove work, or does it create work?

Features that create work must deliver exceptional value. Wherever the system can infer, transcribe, organise or apply information safely, the AI should absorb that work.

## Long-term learning loop

The system should become smarter through:

- Client approvals and rejections.
- Content Run voice notes.
- Campaign and post performance.
- Staff observations.
- Client feedback.
- Reports.
- Structured Skill Cards.
- Updated industry research.

Over time, recommendations should increasingly be supported by the client's own measured results rather than generic opinion.

## Boundaries

- CG Hours remains a separate application because it contains payroll, time and sensitive staff information.
- CG Dynamics must not expose payroll, finance, profit or confidential accounting information to its assistant or client-facing areas.
- Client-specific knowledge should remain tied to active clients and must not pollute general industry knowledge.
- Historical clients must not become default knowledge for active work.
- WhatsApp may remain an important notification and communication entry point during transition, but the app should increasingly become the source of truth for structured approvals and workflows.
