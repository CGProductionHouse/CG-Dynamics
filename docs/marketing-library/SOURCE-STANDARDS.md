# Marketing Library Source Standards

Last updated: 2026-07-01
Status: Active standard

## Purpose

This file defines what counts as acceptable source material for the CG Dynamics Marketing Library.

The goal is to protect the AI Workforce from generic AI marketing noise, weak SEO blogs and unsupported claims.

## Main rule

AI output is not a source.

AI can help summarise, organise and apply knowledge, but the source of truth must be human authored, official, measured or directly captured from real business context.

## Preferred source types

### 1. Foundational books

Use books that have shaped advertising, copywriting, brand strategy, consumer behaviour or behavioural psychology.

Starter list for review:

- Claude Hopkins, Scientific Advertising.
- David Ogilvy, Ogilvy on Advertising.
- Eugene Schwartz, Breakthrough Advertising.
- Gary Halbert, The Boron Letters.
- Joseph Sugarman, The Adweek Copywriting Handbook.
- Robert Collier, The Robert Collier Letter Book.
- Al Ries and Jack Trout, Positioning.
- Robert Cialdini, Influence.
- Daniel Kahneman, Thinking, Fast and Slow.
- Chip Heath and Dan Heath, Made to Stick.
- Jonah Berger, Contagious.
- Rory Sutherland, Alchemy.
- Byron Sharp, How Brands Grow.
- Marty Neumeier, The Brand Gap.

Important:
A book title on this list is not automatically a Skill Card. Each Skill Card must still reference a specific principle, chapter, section or page where possible.

### 2. Official platform documentation

Use official documentation where platform mechanics matter.

Examples:

- Google Search documentation.
- Google Ads documentation.
- Google Business Profile documentation.
- Meta Business documentation.
- Platform measurement and tracking documentation.

### 3. Research and market reports

Use reputable sources for market behaviour, platform usage, consumer trends and South African context.

Acceptable examples:

- Academic research.
- Official statistics.
- Reputable research companies.
- Platform audience reports.
- Industry bodies.
- Transparent case studies with real data.

### 4. Internal campaign data

Internal results are valuable because they show what works in the real business.

Examples:

- Campaign results.
- Reach and engagement patterns.
- Leads and enquiry data.
- Website analytics.
- Search query data.
- Paid ads results.
- Client report observations.

Rules:

- Always record the date range.
- Always record the data source.
- Always record the metric observed.
- Do not treat one campaign result as universal truth.

### 5. Client and staff knowledge

Client and staff knowledge is useful but must be labelled correctly.

Examples:

- Client interview notes.
- Owner preferences.
- Sales objections heard by the client.
- Staff observations from production.
- Account manager notes.

Rules:

- Label as Opinion or Internal Learning unless supported by data.
- Do not present it as proven market evidence.
- Tie client specific knowledge to active client records where possible.

## Weak sources to avoid

Avoid using these as source material:

- Generic AI generated marketing blogs.
- SEO listicles with no data.
- Social media threads with no evidence.
- Unsourced claims about algorithms.
- Engagement bait marketing posts.
- Vague trend articles.
- Any source that mainly repeats words like elevate, unlock, transform, game changing or next level without substance.

## Citation requirement

Every reviewed Skill Card should include:

- Source name.
- Source type.
- Author or organisation.
- Title.
- Year where available.
- Chapter, section, page, URL or report location where available.
- Notes on how the principle was interpreted.

## Evidence labels

Use these labels consistently:

### Proven principle

Supported by foundational sources or strong research.

### Platform rule

Supported by official platform documentation.

### Market observation

Supported by reputable market data or repeated internal data.

### Internal learning

Supported by CG Production House experience or campaign outcomes.

### Client opinion

Captured from an active client or stakeholder. Useful for brand fit, but not universal proof.

### Hypothesis

A reasonable idea that must be tested before being treated as reliable.

## Review standard

Before a Skill Card becomes Active, ask:

1. Is the source real?
2. Is the source appropriate for the claim?
3. Is the card actionable?
4. Is the confidence level honest?
5. Could an AI agent misuse this card?
6. Does this card reduce generic AI output?

If the answer is weak, keep the card in Draft.
