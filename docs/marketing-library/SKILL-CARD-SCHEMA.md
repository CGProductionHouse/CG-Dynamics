# Skill Card Schema

**Status:** Current working standard  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-20

A Skill Card is a reviewed, traceable unit of reusable knowledge that an authorised CG Dynamics agent may apply.

## Identity

- **Card ID:** Stable unique identifier.
- **Title:** Clear human-readable principle.
- **Version:** Increment when meaning or evidence changes.
- **Status:** `draft`, `needs_review`, `verified`, `active`, `restricted`, `retired`.
- **Owner:** Person or team responsible for maintenance.

## Classification

- **Knowledge layer:** Universal, platform, South African, industry, client or performance learning.
- **Category:** Advertising, copywriting, psychology, brand, platform, reporting, compliance or other controlled category.
- **Applicable industries:** Explicit list.
- **Applicable platforms:** Explicit list.
- **Applicable specialist agents:** Explicit list.
- **Region:** Global, South Africa, province, platform region or client-specific.

## Principle

State one clear principle in plain language. A card should not bundle several unrelated rules.

## Why it matters

Explain the business or communication problem the principle helps solve.

## Evidence

- **Primary source ID:** Link to a Source Note.
- **Additional source IDs:** Supporting or contrasting sources.
- **Source location:** Verified chapter, section, page for the exact edition, official page heading or internal record identifier.
- **Evidence type:** Direct source statement, synthesis, measured internal result or approved client truth.
- **Direct support:** What the source directly establishes.
- **Interpretation:** What CG Dynamics infers or translates.

## Confidence and risk

- **Confidence:** Low, Medium or High.
- **Extrapolation risk:** Low, Medium or High.
- **Freshness risk:** Stable, slow-changing, time-sensitive or event-specific.
- **Known limitations:** Sample, market, platform, period or context limitations.
- **Contradicting evidence:** Relevant disagreement that agents must consider.

## Application guidance

Describe how an agent should use the principle in practical work.

Include:

- Suitable scenarios.
- Required client or campaign context.
- Questions to answer before applying it.
- Appropriate output types.
- When human judgement is required.

## Humanisation guidance

Explain how to apply the principle without producing generic AI language.

Where relevant include:

- Natural phrasing patterns.
- Specific details to seek from the client.
- Clichés and empty phrases to avoid.
- Tone risks.
- Examples of evidence-led specificity.

This section guides style but must not become a rigid template repeated across clients.

## Examples

Provide concise examples labelled as:

- Source example.
- Modern translation.
- Industry example.
- Client-specific example.

Client-specific examples must remain permission-scoped and may not be copied into other clients' work.

## Misuse warnings

List common ways the principle becomes manipulative, irrelevant, clichéd, legally risky or overgeneralised.

## Decision logic

Where useful, express lightweight conditions:

- Apply when.
- Do not apply when.
- Ask for human review when.
- Expire or recheck when.

Agents must not treat these conditions as a substitute for contextual judgement.

## Learning connection

Define which outcomes may strengthen, weaken or specialise the card:

- Client approval or rejection.
- Content-run amendments.
- Campaign performance.
- Conversion or enquiry results.
- Repeated staff observations.
- New official platform guidance.
- Contradicting research.

A single result should not rewrite a universal principle. Client-specific patterns should normally update the Client Brain first.

## Activation requirements

A card may become `active` only when:

1. Its source exists and has been reviewed.
2. The claimed principle is traceable to the source.
3. Direct support and interpretation are separated.
4. No quotation or reference is fabricated.
5. Confidence and risk are honestly recorded.
6. Applicable scope is defined.
7. Misuse warnings are present where necessary.
8. A human reviewer approves activation.
9. Changing sources have a future review date.
10. Client-specific information is correctly permission-scoped.

## Agent behaviour requirements

When an agent uses a card, it should be able to provide a concise rationale without dumping internal documentation onto the user.

The agent must:

- Prefer relevant cards over a large quantity of loosely related cards.
- Distinguish authoritative guidance from internal learning.
- Avoid pretending uncertain findings are settled facts.
- Respect expiry and review dates.
- Respect client isolation.
- Never use inactive cards as trusted knowledge.
