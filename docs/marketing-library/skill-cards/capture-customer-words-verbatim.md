# Skill Card — Capture Customer Words Verbatim

Status: Active draft
Domain: Universal Marketing Brain / Market and Customer Understanding

## Principle

Real customer language is evidence. Preserve the original wording before interpreting, summarising or rewriting it.

## Use when

- analysing interviews, calls, reviews, comments or enquiries;
- building a Client Brain;
- writing offers, headlines, FAQs or objection responses;
- identifying recurring emotional or practical tensions.

## Method

For every useful phrase, store:
- exact wording;
- source and date;
- speaker role;
- situation;
- linked trigger;
- linked objection or desired progress;
- privacy and consent status;
- interpretation in a separate field.

Useful phrase patterns often include:
- “I just need…”
- “I’m tired of…”
- “I don’t trust…”
- “What worries me is…”
- “We used to…”
- “I nearly didn’t…”
- “The reason I chose…”

## Why it matters

Customers often reveal the clearest message strategy in ordinary language. Replacing that language too early with corporate wording destroys specificity and emotional truth.

## Misuse warnings

- Do not clean up the original quote in the evidence field.
- Do not invent a quote to dramatise an insight.
- Do not expose names or private conversations without consent and safe handling.
- Do not assume a memorable phrase is a market-wide pattern.
- Do not present adapted copy as a direct quotation.

## Output requirement

The system must be able to show the difference between:
- original phrase;
- interpretation;
- strategic implication;
- adapted marketing copy.
