# Skill Card — Map the Trigger, Not Only the Demographic

Status: Active draft
Domain: Universal Marketing Brain / Market and Customer Understanding

## Principle

People enter a category because something happened. Marketing should identify and address that situation instead of relying only on demographic descriptions.

## Use when

- researching a market;
- planning campaigns;
- selecting content occasions;
- diagnosing weak relevance;
- designing offers or message timing.

## Method

Ask:
1. What happened immediately before the need appeared?
2. Why did it matter at that moment?
3. What practical and emotional state existed?
4. Who else was involved?
5. What was the person doing instead?
6. What made action urgent, acceptable or avoidable?
7. How often does this situation recur?

Store the trigger with its evidence, context, recurrence and confidence.

## Example

Weak:
- Audience: adults aged 25–45 who enjoy eating out.

Stronger:
- Situation: payday Friday, friends have not seen one another for weeks, nobody wants to host, and the group needs an easy place with atmosphere and predictable pricing.

The second description gives the marketer timing, emotion, social context, offer considerations and creative possibilities.

## Misuse warnings

- Do not invent a trigger because it makes a neat campaign.
- Do not treat a seasonal calendar date as proof of customer demand.
- Do not assume all customers in one demographic share the same trigger.
- Do not confuse the trigger with the final product requested.

## Output requirement

A trigger statement should identify:
- event or condition;
- customer state;
- urgency;
- current alternative;
- people involved;
- evidence and confidence.
