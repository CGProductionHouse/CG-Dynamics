# Skill Card — Map Functional, Emotional, Social and Identity Progress

Status: Active draft
Domain: Universal Marketing Brain / Market and Customer Understanding

## Principle

Customers are rarely buying only a functional outcome. Strong research maps the practical, emotional, social and identity progress they are trying to make.

## Four lenses

### Functional
What must become easier, faster, safer, cheaper, clearer or better?

### Emotional
How does the person want to feel before, during and after the decision?

### Social
How do they want to be seen, treated or included by other people?

### Identity
Who are they trying to become, remain or prove themselves to be?

## Method

1. Start with a recent real situation.
2. Record the before state.
3. Record the desired after state.
4. Separate what the customer said from what the researcher inferred.
5. Link every progress statement to evidence and confidence.
6. Check whether the business can credibly support that progress.

## Example

Architecture:
- Functional: obtain a buildable, approved home design.
- Emotional: feel less overwhelmed and more certain.
- Social: show family that the project is under control.
- Identity: become the person who successfully created the family home.

The identity layer does not replace the functional requirement. Both matter.

## Misuse warnings

- Do not invent aspirational identities because they sound sophisticated.
- Do not reduce every purchase to status.
- Do not use identity pressure to shame or manipulate.
- Do not ignore practical barriers while writing emotional copy.
- Do not generalise one person's identity motive to a whole market.

## Output requirement

Each progress statement must include:
- progress type;
- before state;
- desired after state;
- situation;
- evidence state;
- confidence;
- contradictions or limitations.
