## Principle

Advertising should be treated as a sales conversation, not as a display of creativity. The purpose is to persuade someone to take action, not to impress them with style.

## Summary

Hopkins argued that advertising is not a performance art. It is salesmanship multiplied by media. Every advertisement, post, caption or landing page should serve the same function as a salesperson: explain the offer, build trust, overcome objections and ask for the action. If the ad does not do the work of a good salesperson, it is not doing its job.

## Why it matters

This principle protects marketing from becoming self-indulgent. When teams treat advertising as entertainment or brand theatre, they forget to give the audience a reason to act. This card anchors every other marketing decision in a sales mindset rather than a show mindset.

## How to apply it

1. Before writing any ad, caption or landing page, state what specific action the reader should take.
2. Write the ad as if the reader is sitting across from you and you have thirty seconds to earn their business.
3. Remove any sentence that exists to sound clever rather than to persuade.
4. Check every claim against the question: would a good salesperson say this to a prospect?
5. Use the same structure a good salesperson would: attention, interest, desire, action.

## Examples

- A headline that says what the product does and why it matters, not a clever pun or vague benefit.
- A social media caption that opens with a problem the reader recognises and closes with a clear next step.
- A landing page that answers objections in order instead of leading with brand slogans.

## Mistakes to avoid

- Writing ads that look like art projects or contest entries. If the creative work overshadows the offer, the ad has failed.
- Assuming a clever headline replaces a clear value proposition.
- Treating brand awareness as an excuse to avoid asking for the sale.
- Using advertising to impress peers or win industry recognition instead of serving the customer.

## Agent instructions

- Use this card when evaluating whether a piece of copy puts persuasion before presentation.
- Use this card when generating first drafts of ad copy, captions or landing page headlines.
- Do not use this card to justify aggressive or dishonest sales tactics. Salesmanship in this context means respectful persuasion with a clear offer.
- Do not use this card to dismiss brand building. It is a separate function that Hopkins did not write about in depth.
