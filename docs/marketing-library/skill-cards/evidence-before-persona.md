# Skill Card — Evidence Before Persona

Status: Draft for manual review
Universal domain: Market and customer understanding
Durability: Durable principle

## Principle

Do not invent a customer persona and then market to the invention.

Start with observed behaviour, recent customer stories, first-party data and exact customer language. Use profiles only as summaries of evidence.

## Why it matters

Fictional personas often create false confidence. They make strategy appear specific while hiding assumptions, stereotypes and missing research.

A useful audience profile should help the marketer make a decision and should be traceable to evidence.

## Use when

- creating a campaign brief
- defining an industry audience
- building a Client Brain
- deciding content pillars
- writing offers or objections
- segmenting customers

## Required process

1. State the decision the profile must help make.
2. List the evidence available.
3. Separate fact, pattern, interpretation and assumption.
4. Record contradictory evidence.
5. Mark confidence.
6. Create a profile only if it improves a real decision.

## Evidence labels

- Observed: directly seen or recorded behaviour
- Reported: said by a customer or participant
- Measured: supported by behavioural or transaction data
- Inferred: interpretation supported by evidence
- Assumed: not yet verified

The system must never silently convert assumed information into fact.

## Good output

“Recent weekday customers repeatedly mentioned wanting somewhere calm for an informal meeting. This is supported by six interviews and booking notes, but we do not yet know whether the pattern applies to weekend visitors.”

## Bad output

“Our ideal customer is Sarah, a 32-year-old ambitious professional who loves quality experiences and social media.”

## Misuse warnings

- Demographics alone do not prove motive.
- A polished persona is not evidence.
- One loud customer is not automatically a segment.
- Profiles must not encourage stereotyping.
- Client opinions about customers remain assumptions until supported.

## Evaluation checklist

- Can every important claim be traced to evidence?
- Are assumptions visibly labelled?
- Does the profile describe situations and behaviour, not only traits?
- Does it help make a real marketing decision?
- Are contradictions and missing evidence visible?

## Source basis

Authoritative user-research guidance consistently requires teams to learn from actual or likely users, existing evidence, interviews and observation, while treating unsupported opinions as assumptions that require testing.

## Confidence

High for the principle.

The exact amount of evidence needed depends on risk, scope and the decision being made.
