# Skill Card — Ask About the Last Real Event

Status: Draft for manual review
Universal domain: Market and customer understanding
Durability: Durable research principle

## Principle

When trying to understand customer behaviour, ask for a recent specific event rather than a general opinion or hypothetical prediction.

## Why it matters

People simplify, rationalise and idealise their behaviour when answering broad questions.

A real recent event reveals:
- the trigger
- sequence
- people involved
- alternatives considered
- barriers
- emotions
- language
- outcome

## Preferred questions

- Tell me about the last time this happened.
- What was going on immediately before that?
- What did you do first?
- What happened next?
- What were you using or doing before?
- Who else was involved?
- What nearly stopped you?
- What made you decide then rather than later?
- What did you expect would happen?
- How did it turn out?

Use neutral follow-up questions and allow the participant to tell the story in their own order.

## Avoid

- Would you buy this?
- Do you like this idea?
- How important is quality to you?
- Would you use an app that…?
- Don’t you think this would be easier?

These questions often invite politeness, prediction or agreement rather than evidence.

## Example

Weak:
“Do you enjoy going out for breakfast?”

Stronger:
“Tell me about the last time you went out for breakfast. What made that morning different from eating at home?”

Weak:
“Would you hire an architect who offers better communication?”

Stronger:
“Tell me about the last time you needed drawings or professional building advice. How did you decide who to contact?”

## Output structure

For each event capture:
- participant and context
- date or recency
- trigger
- desired progress
- sequence
- alternatives
- people involved
- barriers and anxieties
- exact phrases
- decision
- outcome
- researcher observations
- interpretations kept separately

## Misuse warnings

- One event does not establish a universal pattern.
- Memory can still be incomplete or reconstructed.
- Do not lead participants toward the client’s preferred answer.
- Do not treat a stated reason as the only cause without supporting evidence.
- Protect privacy and obtain appropriate consent for recording and reuse.

## Evaluation checklist

- Was the discussion grounded in a specific recent event?
- Were open and neutral questions used?
- Did the researcher capture sequence and context?
- Were exact customer words preserved?
- Were observation and interpretation separated?
- Were patterns withheld until several cases were compared?
