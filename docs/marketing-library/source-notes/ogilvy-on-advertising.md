# Ogilvy on Advertising

**Author:** David Ogilvy  
**Year published:** 1983  
**Source type:** Foundational book  
**Tier:** 1 — Primary and foundational

## Why this source matters

David Ogilvy is one of the most referenced figures in advertising history. Ogilvy on Advertising covers brand building, direct response, research, creative process, client relationships and agency management. It combines the direct response rigour of Hopkins with brand thinking.

It matters because Ogilvy cared about research, results and craft, and because his core principles are still taught and used in agencies today.

## What it can support

- The importance of research before creative work.
- Headline and visual principles that still apply.
- Brand voice and consistency.
- The concept of the big idea.
- How to write for print, TV and direct mail (with applicable modern translations).
- The value of long-form copy when the audience is interested.
- Positioning by what the product does, not by what the company wants to say.
- Client service and agency professionalism standards.

## What it cannot support

- Digital advertising, social media or influencer strategy.
- Algorithmic feed mechanics, Meta or Google Ads platform specifics.
- SEO, content marketing or search behaviour.
- Video and reel production workflows.
- South African market data or local consumer behaviour.
- Programmatic advertising or modern audience targeting.
- Any claim about current platform costs, click-through rates or conversion benchmarks.
