# Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion

**Author:** Robert Cialdini  
**Year published:** 1984 (revised editions in 2007 and 2021)  
**Source type:** Foundational book / Behavioural psychology  
**Tier:** 1 — Primary and foundational

## Why this source matters

Influence introduced the six universal principles of persuasion that are now standard in marketing, sales and advertising training. The principles are rooted in published research, not opinion, and they have been tested across multiple industries and contexts.

This source is valuable because it gives marketing decisions a foundation in human behaviour rather than trends or intuition.

## What it can support

- The six principles: reciprocity, scarcity, authority, consistency, liking and social proof.
- Why each principle works based on human cognitive shortcuts.
- Ethical applications of persuasion principles in marketing and advertising.
- How to structure offers, calls to action and campaigns around known psychological triggers.
- Training AI agents to check whether a piece of copy or a campaign uses recognised principles or relies on weak claims.
- Evaluating why a campaign might have underperformed based on missing psychological triggers.

## What it cannot support

- Platform specific advertising tactics (Meta, Google, TikTok).
- South African consumer behaviour data.
- Modern digital manipulation or dark pattern criticism (the book predates most of this discussion).
- Copywriting style guides or headline formulas.
- Brand positioning or differentiation strategy.
- Visual design or creative direction.
- Campaign measurement or attribution models.
