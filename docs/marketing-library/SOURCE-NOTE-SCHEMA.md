# Source Note Schema

**Status:** Current working standard  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-20

Use this template for every human, official, academic, measured or internal source considered for the CG Dynamics knowledge system.

## Required metadata

- **Source ID:** Stable unique identifier.
- **Title:** Exact source title.
- **Author / organisation:** Original author or responsible body.
- **Source type:** Book, research paper, official documentation, legislation, dataset, interview, client record, campaign data, staff learning or other.
- **Source tier:** Tier 1, Tier 2 or Tier 3 discovery-only.
- **Publication date:** Where available.
- **Edition / version:** Required where editions materially differ.
- **Canonical location:** ISBN, DOI, official URL or internal record identifier.
- **Region:** Global, South Africa, province, platform region or client-specific.
- **Date acquired:** Date the source entered the library.
- **Last checked:** Required for online and changing sources.
- **Next review date:** Required for platform, legal, calendar and trend-sensitive sources.
- **Reviewer:** Human responsible for verification.
- **Status:** `candidate`, `source_note_draft`, `needs_review`, `verified`, `restricted`, `retired`.

## Why this source matters

Explain the practical reason the source is worth retaining and which decisions it may improve.

## Authority assessment

Record:

- Why the source is credible.
- Whether it is primary or interpretive.
- Known limitations, disputes or commercial incentives.
- Whether it describes universal principles, a specific context or a time-sensitive platform rule.

## What this source can support

List the claims, principles, methods or factual fields that may legitimately be derived from the source.

## What this source cannot support

Explicitly record boundaries to prevent agents from stretching the source beyond its evidence.

## Relevant knowledge layers

Select one or more:

- Universal marketing.
- Consumer psychology.
- Copywriting.
- Brand strategy.
- Platform intelligence.
- South African intelligence.
- Industry Brain.
- Client Brain.
- Performance learning.
- Compliance.

## Relevant industries and agents

List applicable Industry Brains and specialist agents. Avoid assigning a source universally unless it is genuinely universal.

## Verified source locations

For books and long publications:

- Chapter.
- Section.
- Page range for the exact edition, only when verified.

For online sources:

- Official page title.
- Canonical URL.
- Page section or heading.
- Last checked date.

For internal sources:

- Record type.
- Client or campaign identifier where authorised.
- Date range.
- Measurement method.

## Candidate principles

List possible Skill Cards without treating them as approved knowledge.

Each candidate must state:

- Candidate title.
- Direct source support.
- Interpretation or modern translation.
- Expected extrapolation risk.
- Verification still required.

## Quotes and paraphrases

- Direct quotations must be exact, short and traceable.
- Paraphrases must never be presented as quotations.
- No quote or page number may be invented.
- Copyrighted sources should be summarised rather than copied extensively.

## Modern application notes

Clearly separate modern application from the original source context. For example, a historic direct-mail principle may be translated to social media or landing pages, but the translation is not the author's original platform advice.

## Conflicts and contradictions

Record important disagreement with other sources, later research or current platform guidance. Do not silently merge conflicting claims into a false universal rule.

## Access and licensing

Record whether the source may be stored in full, linked only, quoted within limits, summarised, or retained as an internal reference. Do not upload or reproduce copyrighted works without permission.

## Review decision

At review, record:

- Decision.
- Reviewer.
- Date.
- Corrections required.
- Approved candidate cards.
- Rejected candidate cards and reasons.
- Next review date.

## Mandatory safeguards

- AI output is never the authority.
- A polished summary is not verification.
- Client-specific knowledge may not leak into universal cards.
- Former-client information may not become default knowledge.
- Changing online guidance requires revalidation.
- Internal performance observations must include sample size and uncertainty where possible.
