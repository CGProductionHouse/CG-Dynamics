# Platform Expert System Blueprint

Last updated: 2026-07-20
Status: Active Phase 2 foundation

## Purpose

Platform Experts translate durable marketing principles into current platform mechanics. They are living, versioned knowledge systems rather than timeless guides.

The Universal Marketing Brain decides what is strategically and creatively sound. A Platform Expert decides how that work should be adapted, distributed, measured and governed on a specific platform now.

## Required inheritance order

Universal Marketing Brain
→ Platform Expert
→ Industry Expert
→ Client Brain
→ Current brief
→ Execution
→ Performance learning

A platform tactic may not override truthful claims, client identity, commercial logic or audience evidence.

## Required modules for every platform

1. Platform role and user intent
2. Account and surface types
3. Discovery and ranking systems
4. Available content formats
5. Creative adaptation rules
6. Copy, metadata and search discovery
7. Community and conversation behaviour
8. Conversion paths
9. Paid distribution relationship
10. Analytics and diagnostic metrics
11. Policy, eligibility and brand-safety constraints
12. Local and territory limitations
13. Source register
14. Change log
15. Review and expiry policy
16. Atomic Skill Cards
17. Evaluation checklist

## Knowledge states

- verified-current: confirmed by an official source during the current review window;
- observed-current: repeatedly observed in first-party performance but not officially documented;
- experimental: plausible tactic undergoing controlled testing;
- disputed: credible evidence conflicts;
- stale: review window expired;
- retired: feature or rule no longer applies.

## Source hierarchy

### Tier 1

- official help centres;
- official transparency and policy documentation;
- official creator or business education;
- official API and product documentation;
- first-party account analytics and experiments.

### Tier 2

- credible platform representatives;
- established measurement partners;
- rigorous agency or academic research with transparent methods.

### Tier 3

- creator claims;
- social posts;
- newsletters;
- unsourced algorithm summaries.

Tier 3 creates research leads only.

## Mandatory metadata

Every platform principle or Skill Card must record:

- platform;
- surface;
- territory;
- source URL or source reference;
- researched date;
- last verified date;
- expected review interval;
- expiry date or stale rule;
- evidence state;
- confidence;
- account eligibility limitations;
- policy sensitivity;
- change history;
- applicable objective;
- prohibited extrapolation.

## Review cadence

- ranking, recommendation and format rules: every 30 days;
- advertising products and specifications: every 30 days;
- policy and eligibility: every 30 days or immediately after a flagged change;
- stable account setup guidance: every 90 days;
- observed performance rules: after sufficient new first-party data or every campaign cycle.

The system must not silently use stale knowledge. It should either refresh, downgrade confidence or state that current verification is unavailable.

## Core platform reasoning sequence

1. Start with the business objective.
2. Identify the audience situation and desired action.
3. Select the platform role in the campaign.
4. Select the appropriate surface and format.
5. Adapt the idea without weakening its truth or distinctiveness.
6. Check eligibility, policy and technical requirements.
7. Define the meaningful metric before publishing.
8. Capture result and context.
9. Feed platform-specific learning back without misclassifying it as universal truth.

## Shared anti-patterns

- chasing reach without an objective;
- copying one platform's execution unchanged to another;
- treating engagement as business impact;
- inventing an algorithm rule from one successful post;
- hiding weak creative behind format tricks;
- using stale specifications;
- confusing paid delivery with organic recommendation;
- optimising for a metric that does not match the intended action;
- publishing recommendation-ineligible or policy-sensitive content without review.
