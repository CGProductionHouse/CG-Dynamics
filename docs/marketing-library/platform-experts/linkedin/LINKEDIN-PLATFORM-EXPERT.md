## Platform role

LinkedIn is a professional identity, credibility, network, hiring, expertise and business-development platform. It is strongest when a real person or organisation contributes useful professional context rather than publishing generic corporate announcements.

## Core surfaces

- personal profile and posts;
- company Page;
- feed;
- search;
- newsletters and articles where available;
- events;
- jobs;
- direct messages;
- paid campaigns.

## Core Skill Cards

### Start from professional relevance

A post should help the intended professional audience understand, decide, perform, discuss or recognise something. Remove generic inspiration that could come from any company.

### Prefer first-hand knowledge

Use real projects, decisions, mistakes, methods, results, observations and informed opinions. Separate confidential information from publishable learning.

### Give the post one argument

Structure:

1. clear premise or observation;
2. evidence or experience;
3. interpretation;
4. useful implication;
5. relevant invitation to respond or act.

Do not pad one idea into a long post merely because long text is possible.

### Distinguish person-led and company-led communication

Use people for lived expertise, leadership perspective, craft and relationship. Use the company Page for official proof, employer brand, launches, team and institutional updates. Do not impersonate a founder's voice through generic brand copy.

### Treat comments as professional conversation

Respond with substance. Do not use coordinated comment pods, manufactured controversy or empty agreement to manipulate distribution.

### Measure business relevance

Track qualified profile views, relevant follower growth, meaningful comments, direct conversations, event interest, candidate quality, leads, opportunities and assisted influence. Impressions alone do not prove commercial value.

## Safety and governance

- obtain permission for client and employee stories;
- verify claims, credentials and results;
- protect confidential project information;
- distinguish opinion from evidence;
- avoid scraping or unsolicited automation that violates current rules;
- review current advertising, messaging and feature eligibility.

## Official source register

Use current LinkedIn Help, Marketing Solutions, Pages, creator and advertising documentation as Tier 1 sources. Every mechanic, specification and eligibility rule expires after 30 days unless reverified.

## Evaluation

Pass only when the intended professional audience, useful contribution, author identity, proof, confidentiality check and meaningful outcome are explicit.
