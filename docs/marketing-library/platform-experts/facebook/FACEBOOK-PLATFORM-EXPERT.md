# Facebook Platform Expert

Last verified: 2026-07-20
Review interval: 30 days
Status: Active foundation

## Platform role

Facebook is a relationship, community, local discovery, event, information and paid-distribution platform. For many local businesses it connects public content, recommendations, reviews, events, Groups, Messenger, calls, directions and website actions.

## Surface map

### Feed

Use for relevant updates, proof, useful information, community stories, offers, events and media suited to established or recommended audiences.

Official Facebook guidance confirms that Feed is ranked and personalised. Important signals include a person's prior engagement, preferred content type, engagement received by a post and recency. These are examples, not a complete formula.

### Reels and video

Use when motion, demonstration, personality or narrative creates value. Diagnose viewing quality and downstream action rather than celebrating views without context.

### Pages

A Page is a business information and service surface. Core details, hours, location, contact paths, services, menus, prices and current pinned information must remain accurate.

### Groups

Groups are community environments, not free advertising inventory. Participate only where the client has permission, relevance and a genuine contribution.

### Events

Use for real scheduled experiences. Maintain accurate date, time, location, ticket or booking information and update changes quickly.

### Messenger and comments

Treat responses as customer service and research evidence. Response quality, escalation, privacy and record handling matter more than forced engagement.

## Core Skill Cards

### Build for relevance, not engagement bait

Create material people have a reason to discuss, react to or share. Do not request meaningless comments, use misleading prompts or coordinate artificial interaction.

### Use the Page as an operating asset

Before campaign activity verify:

- category and description;
- location and service area;
- opening hours;
- phone, message and website paths;
- menu or services;
- profile and cover identity;
- pinned or featured information;
- admin access and security.

### Separate organic proof from paid distribution

A boosted post or ad can extend delivery, but spend does not repair weak positioning, unclear creative or an invalid offer. Record whether results came from organic, boosted or Ads Manager distribution.

### Design local information for action

For local businesses include the practical information needed to act: location, date, operating hours, booking method, eligibility, availability and clear next step.

### Use events as structured campaigns

An event campaign should include announcement, value, proof, reminders, practical details, live coverage where useful and post-event evidence. Avoid publishing repeated posters without new information.

### Moderate and learn

Classify comments and messages as enquiry, objection, service issue, praise, misinformation, spam or risk. Respond according to policy, then feed recurring language and objections into the Client Brain.

### Measure the intended action

Match metrics to purpose:

- awareness: relevant reach and frequency;
- community: meaningful comments, shares and returning participation;
- information: clicks, detail views, calls, directions or messages;
- event: responses, ticket actions, bookings and attendance;
- conversion: tracked lead, sale or qualified conversation.

## Recommendation eligibility and quality

Facebook states that content allowed on the service may still be ineligible for recommendation. Low-quality, misleading, sensitive or unoriginal content can receive reduced recommendation. Check current Recommendations Guidelines and account status before relying on discovery.

## Anti-patterns

- engagement bait;
- long captions unrelated to the underlying content;
- unoriginal reposting without material value;
- inaccurate Page information;
- posting identical promotional artwork repeatedly;
- treating Groups as broadcast channels;
- using boost spend without objective, audience and conversion tracking;
- ignoring comments after asking for participation.

## Evaluation checklist

Pass only when:

- the business objective is defined;
- the selected surface fits user intent;
- Page information is accurate;
- organic and paid delivery are separated;
- recommendation eligibility is checked;
- practical local details are complete;
- comment and message handling is planned;
- measurement reaches beyond reactions;
- current sources and account limitations are visible.
