Last verified: 2026-07-20
Review interval: 30 days
Status: Active foundation

## Platform role

WhatsApp Business is primarily a permission-based conversation, service, coordination, approval and conversion channel. For CG Production House clients it may be operationally central because customer and client groups already use WhatsApp for enquiries, approvals and updates.

It must not be treated as a free broadcast feed.

## Core surfaces

- one-to-one conversation;
- group conversation;
- Status;
- business profile and catalogue where available;
- click-to-WhatsApp entry points;
- approved messaging through the WhatsApp Business Platform where applicable.

## Core Skill Cards

### Start with permission and expected context

Before proactive messaging, identify how consent was obtained, what type of message is expected, who owns the relationship and how the recipient can stop communication.

Do not import contact lists and begin promotional messaging merely because numbers are available.

### Distinguish service, approval and promotion

Classify every conversation or template as:

- enquiry response;
- customer service;
- operational update;
- approval request;
- reminder;
- transactional information;
- relationship message;
- promotion.

The wording, urgency, automation and consent standard should match the message type.

### Make approval requests decision-ready

For client approvals include:

- what is being approved;
- relevant asset or link;
- exact decision needed;
- deadline and consequence of delay;
- version or campaign reference;
- space for requested changes;
- responsible team member.

Avoid scattered messages that make the final approved version ambiguous.

### Use conversation memory responsibly

Store useful business context, objections, approvals and preferences only where privacy, consent and access controls allow. Do not expose private group content to unrelated client or industry knowledge.

### Escalate rather than hallucinate

When the agent lacks authority, current pricing, availability or policy certainty, it should assign or escalate the conversation instead of inventing an answer.

### Keep automation visibly useful

Automation should reduce delay and repetition. It should identify itself where required, allow human handoff, preserve context and avoid trapping people in irrelevant menu loops.

### Measure resolved outcomes

Track response time, resolution, approval turnaround, qualified enquiries, bookings, conversion, opt-outs, complaints and handoff quality. Message volume is not success.

## Group-specific rules

- confirm who may add members;
- define group purpose;
- limit unnecessary promotional volume;
- protect numbers and personal information;
- avoid sharing confidential client material into the wrong group;
- record approvals with date, sender and asset version;
- distinguish casual reactions from formal approval.

## Official source register

Use current WhatsApp Business Help Centre, WhatsApp Business Platform documentation, Meta business messaging policies, commerce policies, privacy terms and template guidance. API, template, pricing, consent and feature claims expire after 30 days.

## South African considerations

Consent, personal-information handling and direct marketing must be reviewed against applicable South African law and the organisation's legal guidance. The Platform Expert may flag risk but may not present itself as legal counsel.

## Evaluation

Pass only when consent, message class, recipient expectation, requested action, escalation path, privacy handling, approval traceability and meaningful outcome are explicit.
