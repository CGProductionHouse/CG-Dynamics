# Business Understanding Engine

Last updated: 2026-07-20
Status: Active architecture for Domain 01

## Purpose

Domain 01 is not a persona generator. It is an investigation system that helps the AI workforce discover how a business works, why customers buy, why they hesitate, why they return, and what evidence supports each conclusion.

Marketing is an output of this understanding.

## Investigation sequence

1. Business reality
2. Customer situation
3. Trigger
4. Decision roles
5. Current alternative
6. Desired progress
7. Emotional and identity movement
8. Objections and risk
9. Trust and proof
10. Customer language
11. Evidence confidence
12. Open questions

The system may not skip directly from a client name to content ideas.

## 1. Business reality

Capture:
- what the business actually sells;
- what customers are paying to achieve or avoid;
- how the business makes money;
- which products or services matter commercially;
- operational limits;
- recurring strengths;
- recurring service failures;
- reasons customers return;
- alternatives customers compare;
- what the business can credibly prove.

Separate owner belief from customer evidence.

## 2. Customer situation

Study the specific situation in which demand appears:
- what happened immediately before the need;
- where the person was;
- who was present;
- what pressure existed;
- what they had already tried;
- what made the issue important now;
- what would happen if they did nothing.

## 3. Trigger Library

A trigger is the event, condition, deadline, emotion or social situation that moves a person into the category.

Each trigger record must contain:
- trigger label;
- exact situation;
- customer segment or context;
- practical state;
- emotional state;
- urgency;
- people involved;
- current workaround;
- evidence source;
- recurrence;
- confidence;
- date observed;
- review date.

Examples:
- payday;
- visitors arriving;
- too tired to cook;
- bought land;
- family growing;
- renovation failed;
- long commute;
- holiday travel;
- deadline approaching;
- supplier failure.

## 4. Decision-role map

Store roles separately:
- initiator;
- buyer;
- payer;
- user;
- influencer;
- recommender;
- approver;
- blocker.

One person may hold several roles. A campaign aimed at the wrong role may be well written and commercially useless.

## 5. Current Alternative Library

The real competitor may be:
- doing nothing;
- delaying;
- an existing supplier;
- a manual workaround;
- asking a friend;
- a cheaper substitute;
- avoiding the situation;
- solving only part of the problem.

Each alternative should record why it remains attractive and what would need to change before the customer leaves it.

## 6. Progress map

Map four kinds of progress:
- functional: what must become easier, faster, safer or better;
- emotional: how the person wants to feel;
- social: how they want to be seen or treated;
- identity: who they are trying to become or remain.

Identity claims must be evidence-backed. The system may not manufacture flattering identities because they sound strategic.

## 7. Objection Library

Do not store objections as one generic list.

Classify:
- price;
- value uncertainty;
- trust;
- proof gap;
- timing;
- effort;
- complexity;
- switching cost;
- operational risk;
- social risk;
- identity risk;
- previous bad experience;
- approval dependency.

Each objection record must contain:
- verbatim wording where available;
- context;
- decision role;
- stage of decision;
- whether stated or inferred;
- current response;
- proof needed;
- recurrence;
- confidence.

## 8. Customer Language Bank

Store exact real phrases from interviews, reviews, enquiries, comments, calls, search terms, support messages and consented WhatsApp conversations.

Each phrase must retain:
- exact wording;
- source type;
- source reference;
- date;
- situation;
- speaker role;
- sentiment;
- linked trigger;
- linked objection;
- linked desired progress;
- privacy status;
- anonymisation status;
- interpretation stored separately.

The AI may adapt customer language, but may not falsely present adapted language as a direct quote.

## 9. Evidence states

Every claim must be tagged as one of:
- observed;
- reported;
- measured;
- inferred;
- assumed.

Confidence must consider:
- source strength;
- number of independent examples;
- recurrence;
- recency;
- context match;
- contradictions;
- commercial relevance.

Assumptions may create research questions. They may not become activated truths.

## 10. Required outputs before strategy

A client or industry study should produce:
- business reality summary;
- decision-role map;
- trigger library;
- current alternatives;
- progress map;
- objection library;
- customer language bank;
- trust and proof inventory;
- evidence ledger;
- contradictions;
- unknowns;
- priority research questions.

## Activation rule

No campaign recommendation should be described as evidence-led unless it traces back to at least one evidence record and clearly states the confidence level.

The strongest work should trace:

Evidence
→ Insight
→ Commercial implication
→ Strategic choice
→ Creative direction
→ Execution
→ Performance result
→ Learning
