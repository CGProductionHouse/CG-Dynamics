# Skill Card — Confidence Must Match the Evidence

Domain: Market and customer understanding
Status: Active

## Principle

The certainty of a claim may never exceed the strength, relevance and recurrence of its evidence.

## Confidence scale

### Assumption
No direct evidence. Use only to create a research question.

### Emerging
One or a few relevant signals. Useful for exploration, not broad activation.

### Moderate
Repeated evidence in a defined context with known limitations.

### Strong
Multiple independent and relevant sources, repeated behaviour or measured outcomes, and no unresolved contradiction that changes the conclusion.

## Confidence inputs

- evidence type;
- source quality;
- sample size;
- recurrence;
- recency;
- directness;
- context match;
- contradictions;
- measurement quality.

## Output language

Use calibrated wording:

- “We need to test whether…” for assumptions.
- “Early evidence suggests…” for emerging insights.
- “Evidence in this segment indicates…” for moderate confidence.
- “Repeated first-party evidence shows…” only for strong confidence.

## Misuse warnings

- Do not convert several weak sources into strong evidence by counting them.
- Do not use precise percentages from informal samples.
- Do not generalise a client-specific result to an industry.
- Do not keep confidence unchanged when the context or platform changes.

## Evaluation

Pass when the wording, permitted use and review date all match the confidence level.
