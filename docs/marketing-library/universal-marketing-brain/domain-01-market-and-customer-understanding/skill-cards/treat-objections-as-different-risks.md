# Skill Card — Treat Objections as Different Risks

Domain: Market and customer understanding
Status: Active

## Principle

An objection is a signal about perceived risk, uncertainty, effort or constraint. It is not automatically a request for a lower price.

## Classification

Classify each objection as one or more of:

- affordability;
- value uncertainty;
- trust;
- proof gap;
- timing;
- complexity;
- effort;
- switching cost;
- social risk;
- identity risk;
- operational constraint;
- authority or approval;
- fear of regret.

## Method

1. Preserve the exact objection.
2. Record who raised it and at what decision stage.
3. Ask what consequence the person fears.
4. Identify evidence that would reduce that specific uncertainty.
5. Test whether the objection is genuine, incomplete or a polite refusal.
6. Record whether it was resolved and what changed.

## Response matching

- price pressure may require scope, financing or value explanation;
- trust concerns require credible proof;
- complexity concerns require simplification or guided onboarding;
- timing constraints require sequencing, not fake urgency;
- social risk may require consensus proof or stakeholder involvement.

## Misuse warnings

- Do not argue with the customer.
- Do not use discounts as a universal answer.
- Do not pretend every objection can be overcome.
- Do not hide operational limits.

## Evaluation

Pass when the proposed response reduces the actual risk rather than merely increasing persuasion pressure.
