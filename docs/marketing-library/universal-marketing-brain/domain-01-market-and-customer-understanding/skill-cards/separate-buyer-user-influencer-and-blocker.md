# Skill Card — Separate Buyer, User, Influencer and Blocker

Domain: Market and customer understanding
Status: Active

## Principle

Do not assume the person who speaks first, pays, uses the product and approves the decision is the same person.

## Use when

- preparing a brief;
- analysing a sale or lost lead;
- designing a message sequence;
- mapping B2B, family, group or high-value decisions.

## Required roles

Record separately where relevant:

- initiator;
- buyer;
- payer;
- user;
- influencer;
- recommender;
- approver;
- blocker.

One person may hold several roles. A role may also be absent or unknown.

## Method

1. Start with a recent real purchase or enquiry.
2. Ask who first raised the need.
3. Ask who researched options.
4. Ask who would use or experience the result.
5. Ask who controlled budget or permission.
6. Ask whose concerns could stop the decision.
7. Record the message, proof and risk relevant to each role.

## Output

For each role store person or group, evidence, objective, anxiety, influence strength, stage of involvement and preferred proof.

## Misuse warnings

- Do not invent a committee because the category is B2B.
- Do not assume the payer has the strongest emotional motive.
- Do not merge spouse, owner, manager, financier or regulator into a single persona.
- Unknown roles remain unknown until researched.

## Evaluation

Pass only when the recommendation explains which role it addresses and why.
